Class overview

Our resin art classes in Katy, TX are built around safety, consistency, and creative freedom. Students learn setup standards, resin ratios, and environmental controls that reduce common mistakes such as bubbles, clouding, and uneven cures. After safety and prep, classes move into pigment blending, directional flow, and layered pours for geode, ocean, and abstract styles. Every project includes guided finishing so your final pieces are durable, clean, and display-ready for home décor or gifts.

What you'll learn

  • Safe handling practices, ventilation awareness, and workspace preparation
  • Measuring and mixing resin accurately for reliable curing
  • Pigment blending, lacing, and cell effects for dynamic surface movement
  • Layering methods for geode, ocean-inspired, and abstract resin compositions
  • Top-coat and finishing techniques for a polished, professional look

How the class works

Safety & Prep

You begin with workspace setup, safety protocols, and material planning before resin is mixed.

Pour & Shape

Students execute guided pours while learning flow direction, color placement, and effect-building techniques.

Cure & Finish

Final stages cover curing checks, edge cleanup, and finishing details that elevate the final piece.

Frequently asked questions

Are resin classes safe for beginners?

Yes. Safety instruction is built into every class, including setup, handling, and curing guidance before project work begins.

Can I make a gift-ready project in class?

Yes. Many students create décor pieces, trays, and artwork suitable for gifting, depending on the selected project type.

Do I need prior painting experience?

No prior painting experience is required. We teach process, materials, and design flow from the ground up.

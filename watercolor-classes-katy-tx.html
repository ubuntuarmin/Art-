Class overview

Our watercolor classes in Katy, TX help students understand the core challenge of watercolor: timing water, pigment, and paper response. Lessons combine foundational drills with complete guided projects so you learn both technique and decision-making. Students practice wet-on-wet transitions, controlled glazing, and brush precision while learning how to preserve highlights and avoid muddy passages. From first-time painters to experienced artists seeking cleaner, more luminous results, this workshop offers a clear path to better watercolor outcomes.

What you'll learn

  • Wet-on-wet and wet-on-dry methods for soft transitions and crisp detail
  • Glazing strategy for depth without chalkiness or overworked color
  • Value planning and shape simplification for stronger compositions
  • Brush control for edges, negative painting, and focal-point refinement
  • Finishing workflow for balanced highlights, contrast, and visual harmony

How the class works

Paper & Palette Setup

Class begins with paper preparation, mixing strategy, and tonal sketches to guide your painting decisions.

Layer Development

Students build transparent layers in sequence, learning when to pause, lift, and rework safely.

Detail & Critique

You finish with selective detail, edge control, and critique feedback that improves your next project.

Frequently asked questions

Is watercolor hard for beginners?

Watercolor has a learning curve, but beginners do very well with guided instruction, repeatable exercises, and structured project flow.

What age group can attend watercolor classes?

Classes are open to multiple age groups depending on session format. Contact us to choose the best fit for kids, teens, or adults.

Will I finish artwork during class?

Yes. Many students complete a finished piece or a major stage of a piece in each session, depending on project complexity.
